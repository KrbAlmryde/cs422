Meteor: A small body of matter from outer space that enters the earth's atmosphere, becoming incandescent as a result of friction and appearing as a streak of light.

Meteorite: A meteor that survives its passage through the earth's atmosphere such that part of it strikes the ground. More than 90 percent of meteorites are of rock, while the remainder consist wholly or partly of iron and nickel.

Meteorid: A small body moving in the solar system that would become a meteor if it entered the earth's atmosphere.

"Macrometeorites: the largest meteorites throughout history" is an interactive visualization developed by Roxana Torre to show the incidence of meteorites through time across the world. Through the interface it is possible to explore meteorites according to the year in which they were found, their material, and the location in which they were found. While there have been more than 45700 recorded meteorite landing, approximately 3800 have a mass larger than 1kg. The focus, then, of this visualization is on these "Macrometerorites".

There are two categories of "Macrometerorite" identified in this visualization. Those classified as Falls,which have been observed while they transited the atmosphere or impacting the earth, and those called Finds, which had been discovered after they have impacted earth.

Meteorites can be further divided into three types: Stonymeteorites which are rocks, iron meteorites and stony-iron meteorites which contain both metallic and rocky material. Very special cases are a few meteorites which happen to come from the Moon and Mars.
