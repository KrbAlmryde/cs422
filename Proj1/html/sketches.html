The Good

The visualization does a nice job of providing the user with a lot of information and data without overwhelming them. When any data point clicked, a link to the The Meteoritical Society's page with more detailed information about that particular meteorite is shown.

When moused over, each data point provides a pop-up with relevant information about that particular meteorite including it's name, type, mass, and year found. Some of the more well known meteorites also contain a detailed writeup giving the history and significance of the individual meteorite in question.

The visualization also makes good use of size as a means of representing differences in terms of distinguishing a particular meteorite's mass.

It is easy for the user to filter data by year, Find vs Fall, Type, or Source, allowing for a broad data exploration and offers a more detailed look at specific aspects of the data.

TLDR

  • Clicking on a data point provides a direct link to the Meteoritical Society Page about that particular meteor
  • Detailed data exploration tool
  • Nice filtering capabilities for the data
  • Use of size in data markers to disambiguate size differences between meteorites (larger the maker larger the space rock)
