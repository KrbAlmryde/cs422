Shower Head

An advanced shower head with unknown water saving properties. Has the following features:

  • Twisting dial-like control mechanism used to adjust the spray of water. There are N spray options:
  • grooves along the side which make it easy to grip and turn
  • a small lip is included which enables easier adjustments.
  • Rain setting consists of several nodes spraying long thin streams of water in an wide area. Good for covering the largest area with the highest volume of water. Pressure tends to be lower as there are many streams of water active.
  • Massage setting consists of relatively small set of nodes spraying water in controlled high pressure bursts, somewhat akin to a machine gun. Good for massaging sore muscles, pretending you have a machine gun, and removing dirt.
  • Adjustable head which enables control over the direction of water flow

    • Control Knob

    Controls for the volume and temperature of water

  • Activates water flow by tilting knob upward. Volume is controlled by tilting the head up or down
  • Adjusts water for warm temperature by turning knob counter-clockwise, so that the arrow is pointing towards the red color band
  • Adjusts water to be cooler by turning knob clockwise, pointing arrow towards the blue color band

    • Water Spout

    Primary output source of water. Features a diverter rivet which controls the direction of the water. That is, whether the water goes to the primary output source (water spout) or the secondary output source (shower head)

  • Pull rivet up while water is actively flowing to divert the water to the shower head
  • Push rivet down (default state) to divert water to the primary output spout

    • Bathtub plug

    Action Sequences

    Shower Sequences

  • Enter Bathtub/shower
  • Turn control knob to initiate flow of water
  • Adjust control knob to desired temperature
  • Wait for water to reach desire temperature
  • Test water to ensure desired temperature
  • Re-test water to ensure desire temperature
  • Activate diverter by pulling lever up
  • Verify that water is being diverted to secondary output component (shower head)
  • Forget that pipes leading to shower head have not been flushed and suffer the brutal onslaught of frigid water

    Use-Cases and Activities

    Shower Use-cases

  • Washing ones body

    Obviously the most common use-case involved when using a device such as a shower is for the purposes of cleaning ones body.

  • Relaxation

    At least for me, often times when Ive had a really stressful day, I will use the shower as a method of relaxation. The steam from the hot water has an almost cleansing effect, allowing me to take my mind off the stressful day. This is of course a terrible waste of water so I dont use this method often

  • Holistic care

    Related to relaxation, often when I feel ill, such as when I have a headache, I use the shower as a means of holistic care. For reasons I dont fully understand, Ive found it can often relive (at least temporarily) my ailments

  • Giving the dog a bath

    Contrary to the term above, when washing my dog, the bath is merely the receptacle in which to contain my dog. I use the shower to clean her. Living in an apartment and having no access to an outdoor hose, Ive found that this is an efficient method of maximizing the coverage of water-to-dog ratio when rinsing the soap and grim from her.

    • Bath Use-cases

  • Relaxation

    The primary use I have for taking a bath is for relaxation. There is nothing better after a particularly long day than a soak in the tub.

  • Brewing Beer

    At least one occasion I may or may not have used the bathtub as a means of fermenting alcohol for the purposes of consumption

  • Entertainment

    'Bath time' as a child was more like playtime than it was a period to clean ones body. Add the collection of action-figures and a mountain of bubbles, and you had yourself hours of fun! Mom and Dad were frequently annoyed by this.

  • Laundry

    As a starving graduate student and a Chicago apartment dweller to boot, Ive found myself on more than one occasion lacking the available funds to wash my clothes in a traditional receptacle. When considering what a washing machine actually consists of: a vat of water, detergent, and a mechanism to aggitate the clothing in the water to perfuse the detergent into the clothes, you realize the bathtub makes a suitable replacement in a pinch.

  • Warmth

    Early on in my tenure at Chicago, I found myself suffering from the bitterly cold winter. To combat this, I would fill the tub with warm water and soak up the warmth. This activity was also performed in conjunction with actually cleaning and/or relaxing, but it was just as important on its own as any of the other use cases.

    Viewing Data

    Race, Gender, and Age

    By default, a bar graph consisting of the entire city demographics will be displayed. As Districts and communities are selected, charts will appear allowing the user to compare between city,district, and community level data.

    Each chart consists of a grouping of the major racial demographics organized by age

    Groups are labeled in the following manner:

      NHW: Non Hispanic, White
      NHB: Non Hispanic, Black
      NHAM: Non Hispanic, Native Indian/Alakan
      NHAS: Non Hispanic, Asian
      HISP: Hispanic/Latino
      MULTI: Multiracial
      OTHER: Other

    View by Gender

    The user may choose which gender to look at simply by clicking the "Change Gender" Button. By default, all charts start at the combined level data, switching to Men, and then Women respectively

    Trends

    The most obvious trends one can see are just how segregated the city of chicago is by race. The most obvious example of this would be South Side Chicago district vs Far North Side Chicago district, where the populations of White and African Americans clearly mirror each other. Further more, specific demographics tend to dominate certain communities over others. This can be seem in places like LakeView, which has a high concentration of young, mid 20s to early 30s, white residents.

    Data
